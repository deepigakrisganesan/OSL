# OSL

Ontario Sunshine List published on a calendar year basis, contains the list of all public sector employees earning more than 100K CAD. This list is published as per the public sector salary disclosure act to provide greater transparency and accountability 

In this project using POWER BI as a business analysis tool, the Ontario Sunshine list containing the salary, names, positions and entity of individuals for the last three calendar years 2023- 2025 were cleaned, grouped and analyzed to extract key findings.

Key Findings

Across the 2023-2025 period, the top executive from Ontario Power Generation reported the highest salary of 1.9 million and 2.01 million in 2025 and 2024 respectively. Other executives from Ontario Power Generation also occupy the top spots in earnings list, relative to the spots occupied by top earners from other public sector entities.
The average salary of a public sector employee listed in the Ontario Sunshine List in 2025 is around $134k. This is a marginal increase of just 1.31% over 2024 average salaries. The Consumer Price Index for 2025 persisted at 2.1%. Inflation outpacing the salary increase has reduced the purchasing power of employees in the Sunshine list.
Total Payroll adding up all Sunshine employees’ salaries stands at $54 billion in 2025, making about 4.4% of Ontario’s GDP or 1 in 23 dollars of Ontario’s GDP.
Of all sectors, school boards had the highest employee count but with the lowest median salary. In contrast, Ontario Power Generation reported the highest median salary, despite having a much lower employee count.Across years, this pattern has held making Ontario Power Generation a stand out case with a smaller workforce but a higher median compensation year over year.
School Boards also reported very low median taxable benefits, while Ontario Power Generation had a much higher median taxable benefit across the years
Following Ontario Power Generation, universities as a sector report the next highest median salary, highlighting the higher earning power of University employees over those in school boards.
